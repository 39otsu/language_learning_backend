</ul>
<h3>To further improve your Japanese pronunciation, we suggest you do the following:</h3>
<ul>
<li><span class='bold'>Practice, practice, practice:</span> say the words and phrases you’re learning out loud as much as possible.
Repeat everything you hear, paying attention to the intonation.
<li><span class='bold'>Master the vowels</span> as every letter in Japanese except one (N) contains a vowel.
They are terse vowels, pronounced clearly and sharply. The five vowels are:
<div>
<ul>
<li><b>あ</b>　A (as in father)
<li><b>い</b>　I (as in we)
<li><b>う</b>　U (as in soon)
<li><b>え</b>　E (as in get)
<li><b>お</b>　O (as in old)
</ul>
</div>
<li><span class='bold'>Internalize patterns of intonation through repetitive listening:</span>

is rather to grow your intuitions for natural Japanese rhythm and pitch fluctuation.
